Q.   A housing subdivision contains only two types of homes: ranch-style homes and townhomes. There are twice as many townhomes as ranch-style homes. There are 3 times as many townhomes with pools than without pools. What is the probability that a home selected at random from the subdivision will be a townhome with a pool?

  1. 1/6

  2. 1/5

  3. 1/4

  4. 1/3

  5. 1/2

Solution:Since there are twice as many townhomes as ranch-style homes, the probability of selecting a townhome is 2/3. Now, "there are 3 times as many townhomes with pools than without pools." So the probability that a townhome will have a pool is 3/4.
Hence, the probability of selecting a townhome with a pool is

 2 
 3 
 × 
 3 
 4 
 = 
 1 
 2 

The answer is (E).
