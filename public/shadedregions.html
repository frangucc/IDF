Shaded Regions

To find the area of the shaded region of a figure, subtract the area of the unshaded region from the area of the entire figure.

Example:
What is the area of the shaded region formed by the circle and the rectangle in the figure below?

  1. 15 – 2π

  2. 15 – π

  3. 14

  4. 16 – π

  5. 15π

Solution: To find the area of the shaded region subtract the area of the circle from the area of the rectangle:

area of rectangle – area of circle

3 × 5  – π × 12

15 – π

The answer is (B).
