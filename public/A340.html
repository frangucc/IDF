Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

x + y > 5 and x < 5

Column A
y

Column B
0

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: Since x is less than 5, we can observe that for x + y to be greater than or equal to 5, y must be greater than 0.
Hence, the answer is A. This can be shown explicitly as follows:

Subtracting x from both sides of the inequality x + y > 5 yields y > 5 – x

Now, let’s build the term 5 – x out of the inequality x < 5. To this end, subtract x from both sides of the inequality. This yields

0 < 5 – x

Rewriting this inequality with 5 – x on the left and 0 on the right yields

5 – x > 0 Note that the inequality symbol still points toward 0, so the statement of the inequality has not changed. In general, if x < y, then y > x. In other words, the inequality symbol always points toward the smaller number, regardless of whether the symbol points left or right.

Substituting this result into the inequality y > 5 – x yields

y > 5 – x > 0

By the transitive property of inequalities, y > 0.
Hence, Column A is greater, and the answer is A.
