Q.   At 1 PM, Ship A leaves port traveling 15 mph. Three hours later, Ship B leaves the same port in the same direction traveling 25 mph. At what time does Ship B pass Ship A?

  1. 8:30 PM

  2. 8:35 PM

  3. 9 PM

  4. 9:15 PM

  5. 9:30 PM

Solution:Let t be time that Ship B has been traveling. Then t + 3 is time that Ship A has been traveling. The distance traveled by Ship B is D = rt = 25t, and Ship A’s distance is 15(t + 3). At the moment Ship B passes Ship A, they will have traveled the same distance.
Hence,

25t = 15(t + 3)

25t = 15t + 45

10t = 45

t = 4.5

Since Ship B left port at 4 PM and overtook Ship A in 4.5 hours, it passed Ship A at 8:30 PM. The answer is (A).
