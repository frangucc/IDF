Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

Column A
The average of four positive numbers of which the greatest is 21

Column B
The average of four positive numbers of which the greatest is 11

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: There is not enough information. For example, if the four numbers in Column A are 18, 19, 20, and 21, then their average is 19.5. Further, if the four numbers in Column B are 8, 9, 10, and 11, then their average is 9.5. In this case, Column A is larger. However, keeping the same four numbers for Column B and changing the numbers in Column A to 1, 2, 3, and 21 gives an average of 6.75. In this case, Column B is larger. The answer is (D).
