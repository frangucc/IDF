Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.x + y + z = 10 and
x – y – z = 8

Column A
x

Column B
y

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: Unless a system of equations has at least as many equations as variables, it is unlikely to have a unique solution. Since we have three variables—x, y, and z—and only two equations, there probably is not a unique solution to this system. If x = 9, y = 0, and z = 1, then both equations are satisfied and Column A is larger. However, if x = 9, y = 10, and z = –9, then both equations are satisfied and Column B is larger. This is a double case and therefore the answer is (D).
