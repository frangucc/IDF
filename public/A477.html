Q.   A town has a population growth rate of 10% per year. The population in 1990 was 2000. What was the population in 1992?

  1. 1600

  2. 2200

  3. 2400

  4. 2420

  5. 4000

Solution: Since the population increased at a rate of 10% per year, the population of any year is the population of the previous year + 10% of that same year.
Hence, the population in 1991 is the population of 1990 + 10% of the population of 1990:

2000 + 10% of 2000 =

2000 + 200 =

2200

Similarly, the population in 1992 is the population of 1991 + 10% of the population of 1991:

2200 + 10% of 2200 =

2200 + 220 =

2420

Hence, the answer is (D).
