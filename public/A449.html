Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

x + y = √2xy

Column A
x

Column B
y

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: The only information we have to work with is the equation x + y = √2xy. Since radicals are awkward to work with, let’s square both sides of this equation to eliminate the radical:

(x + y)2 = (2√xy)2

Applying the Perfect Square Trinomial Formula to the left side and simplifying the right side yields

x2 + 2xy + y2 = 4xy

Subtracting 4xy from both sides yields

x2 − 2xy + y2 = 0

Using the Perfect Square Trinomial Formula again yields

(x − y)2 = 0

Taking the square root of both sides yields

√(x − y2)2  = ± √0

Simplifying yields

x – y = 0

Finally, adding y to both sides yields

x = y
The answer is (C).
