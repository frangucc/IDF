COMPLETE FACTORING

When factoring an expression, first check for a common factor, then check for a difference of squares, then for a perfect square trinomial, and then for a general trinomial.

Example:
Factor the expression 2x3 − 2x2 − 12x completely.

Solution:First check for a common factor: 2x is common to each term. Factoring 2x out of each term yields 2x(x2 − x2 − 6). Next, there is no difference of squares, and x2 − x − 6 is not a perfect square trinomial since x does not equal twice the product of the square roots of x2 and 6. Now, –3 and 2 are factors of –6 whose sum is –1. Hence, 2x(x2 − x2 − 6) factors into 2x(x – 3)(x + 2).
