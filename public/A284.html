Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.


O is the center of the circle, and the radius of the circle is 5.

Column A
The circumference of the circle

Column B
The perimeter of Square PQRS

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution:The shortest distance between two points is along the line joining them. So, the lengths of the arcs PQ, QR, RS, and SP are greater than the lengths of the sides PQ, QR, RS, and SP, respectively. The circum ference of the circle is the sum of lengths of the arcs PQ, QR, RS, and SP, and the perimeter of the square is the sum of the sides PQ, QR, RS, and SP. Since each arc is greater than the corresponding side, the circum ference of the circle must be greater than the perimeter of the square.
Hence, the answer is A.
