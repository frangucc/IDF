Q. In the game of chess, the Knight can make any of the moves displayed in the diagram to the right. If a Knight is the only piece on the board, what is the greatest number of spaces from which not all 8 moves are possible?

  1. 8

  2. 24

  3. 38

  4. 48

  5. 56

Solution: Since we are looking for the greatest number of spaces from which not all 8 moves are possible, we can eliminate the greatest number, 56. Now, clearly not all 8 moves are possible from the outer squares, and there are 28 outer squares not 32. Also, not all 8 moves are possible from the next to outer squares, and there are 20 of them not 24. All 8 moves are possible from the remaining squares.
Hence, the answer is 28 + 20 = 48. The answer is D. Notice that 56, (32 + 24), is given as an answer-choice to catch those who don’t add carefully.
