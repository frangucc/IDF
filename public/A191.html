Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

The larger circle and the smaller circle are tangent to each other at A, and O is the center of the larger circle.

Column A
–5

Column B
h

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: Recall that the slope of a line is the rise over the run: m = 
 Δy 
 Δx 
. Using the point (3, –2) and the origin, (0, 0), to calculate the slope of the line gives

m = 
 Δy 
 Δx 
 = 
 −2 − 0 
 3 − 0 
 = 
 −2 
 3 

Calculating the slope between the point (6, h) and the point (3, –2) yields

m = 
 Δy 
  Δx 
 = 
 h − (−2) 
 6 − 3 
 = 
 h + 2 
 3 

Since the point (6, h) is on the line, the slope between (6, h) and (3, –2) is also –2/3:

 h + 2 
 3 
 = 
 −2 
 3 

  h + 2 = –2

        h = –4

Hence, Column B is larger, and the answer is (B).
