Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

Column A
The surface area of a cone with radius 3

Column B
The surface area of a cone with height 3

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: Since we don’t know the radius of the cone in Column A, the cone can be as wide or as narrow as we want.
Hence, the surface area can be a large or small as we want. Similarly, since we don’t know the height of the cone in Column B, the surface area can be as large or small as we want.
Hence, there is not enough information to decide which column is larger. The answer is (D).
