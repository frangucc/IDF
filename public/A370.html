Q. Let x and y be prime numbers such that x > y. If q = x/y, then q must be

  1. An integer greater than one.

  2. An integer less than one.

  3. A fraction less than one.

  4. A fraction greater than one.

  5. An even number.

Solution: Since x and y are prime numbers and x > y, we know that x > y > 0. Dividing this inequality by y yields x/y > y/y > 0/y. Reducing yields x/y > 1. Since x and y are prime numbers, they will not have any common factors that could reduce x/y to an integer.
Therefore, x/y is an irreducible fraction greater than one. The answer is D.
