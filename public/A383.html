Q.   2x + y = 3

3y = 9 – 6x

How many solutions does the above system of equations have?

  1. None

  2. One

  3. Two

  4. Four

  5. An infinite number

Solution:Start with the bottom equation 3y = 9 – 6x:

Dividing by 3 yields y = 3 – 2x

Adding 2x yields 2x + y = 3

Notice that this is the top equation in the system.
Hence, the system is only one equation in two different forms. Thus, there are an infinite number of solutions. For example, the pair x = 2, y = –1 is a solution as is the pair x = 0, y = 3. The answer is (E).
