Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

3x + y < 4

x > 3

Column A
0

Column B
y

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: Subtracting 3x from both sides of 3x + y < 4 yields y < 4 – 3x. Now, multiplying both sides of x > 3 by –3 yields –3x < –9. Adding 4 to both sides yields 4 – 3x < –5. Now, using the transitive property to combine y < 4 – 3x and 4 – 3x < –5 yields y < 4 – 3x < –5.
Hence, y < –5. In other words, y is negative.
Hence, Column A is larger. The answer is A.
