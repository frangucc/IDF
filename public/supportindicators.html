Support Indicators

Supporting words support or further explain what has already been said. These words often introduce synonyms for words elsewhere in the sentence. Following are some common supporting words:

AND

FURTHERMORE

IN ADDITION

ALSO

LIKEWISE

FOR

INDEED

SIMILARLY

TRULY

Example :
Davis is an opprobrious and                 speaker, equally caustic toward friend or foe—a true curmudgeon.

  1. lofty

  2. vituperative

  3. unstinting

  4. retiring

  5. laudatory

Solution:"And" in the sentence indicates that the missing adjective is similar in meaning to "opprobrious," which is very negative. Now, vituperative—the only negative word—means "abusive." Hence, the answer is (B).

Example :
The belief that sanctions and tactical military strikes can turn the people of a country against a dictator is folly; indeed, as we are witnessing in the Balkans, this                 causes the population to rally around the dictator.

  1. sometimes

  2. rarely

  3. invariably

  4. never

  5. occasionally

Solution:"Indeed" in the sentence indicates that the second clause supports and emphasizes what is stated in the first clause: that sanctions and tactical military strikes will not work. Now, something that will not work will invariably (always) fail. The answer is (C).
