Compass Headings

In this type of problem, typically two people are traveling in perpendicular directions. The key to these problems is often the Pythagorean Theorem.

Example:
At 1 PM, Ship A leaves port heading due west at x miles per hour. Two hours later, Ship B is 100 miles due south of the same port and heading due north at y miles per hour. At 5 PM, how far apart are the ships?

  1. √(4x)2 + (100 + 2y)2

  2. x + y

  3. √x2 +  y2

  4. √(4x)2 + (2y)2

  5. √(4x)2 + (100 − 2y)2

Solution:Since Ship A is traveling at x miles per hour, its distance traveled at 5 PM is D = rt = 4x. The distance traveled by Ship B is D = rt = 2y. This can be represented by the following diagram:

Applying the Pythagorean Theorem yields s2 = (4x)2 + (100 − 2y)2 . Taking the square root of this equation gives s = √(4x)2 + (100 − 2y)2 . The answer is (E).
