Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

Column A
z – x

Column B
y

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution:In a triangle, the sum of lengths of any two sides is greater than the length of the third side.
Hence, x + y > z. Subtracting x from both sides of this inequality yields y > z – x.
Hence, Column B is greater than Column A. The answer is B.
