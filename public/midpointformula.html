Midpoint Formula

The midpoint M between points (x,y) and (a,b) is given by

M = (
 x + a 
 2 
 , 
 y + b 
 2 
)

In other words, to find the midpoint, simply average the corresponding coordinates of the two points.

Example:
In the figure below, polygon PQRO is a square and T is the midpoint of side QR. What are the coordinates of T ?

  1. (1, 1)

  2. (1, 2)

  3. (1.5, 1.5)

  4. (2, 1)

  5. (2, 3)

Solution:Since point R is on the x-axis, its y-coordinate is 0. Further, since PQRO is a square and the x-coordinate of Q is 2, the x-coordinate of R is also 2. Since T is the midpoint of side QR, the midpoint formula yields

T = (
 2 + 2 
 2 
,
 2 + 0 
 2 
) = (
 4 
 2 
,
 2 
 2 
) = (2,1)

The answer is (D).
