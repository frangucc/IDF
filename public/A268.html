Q. A square with sides of length 3 is intersected by a line at S and T. What is the maximum possible distance between S and T?

  1. √6

  2. 2√3

  3. 3√2

  4. 2√5

  5. 9

Solution:The maximum possible distance between S and T will occur when the line intersects the square at opposite vertices:

Hence, the maximum distance is the length of the diagonal of the square. Applying the Pythagorean Theorem yields

ST2 = 32 + 32

ST2 = 18

ST =  √18 = 3√2
The answer is C.
