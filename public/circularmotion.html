Circular Motion

In this type of problem, the key is often the arc length formula S = Rθ, where S is the arc length (or distance traveled), R is the radius of the circle, and θ is the angle.

Example:
If a wheel is spinning at 1200 revolutions per minute, how many revolutions will it make in t seconds?

  1. 2t

  2. 10t

  3. 20t

  4. 48t

  5. 72t

Solution:Since the question asks for the number of revolutions in t seconds, we need to find the number of revolutions per second and multiply that number by t. Since the wheel is spinning at 1200 revolutions per minute and there are 60 seconds in a minute, we get
 1200 revolutions 
 60 seconds 
 = 20 rev/sec . Hence, in t seconds, the wheel will make 20t revolutions. The answer is (C).
