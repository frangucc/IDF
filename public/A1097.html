Q. In the following sentence, part or all of the sentence is underlined. The answer-choices offer five ways of phrasing the underlined part. If you think the sentence as written is better than the alternatives, choose A, which merely repeats the underlined part; otherwise choose one of the alternatives.

Traveling across and shooting the vast expanse of the Southwest, in 1945 Ansel Adams began his photographic career.

  1. Traveling across and shooting the vast expanse of the Southwest, in 1945 Ansel Adams began his photographic career.

  2. In 1945, Ansel Adams began his photographic career, traveling across and shooting the vast expanse of the Southwest.

  3. Having traveled across and shooting the vast expanse of the Southwest, in 1945 Ansel Adams began his photographic career.

  4. Ansel Adams, in 1945 began his photographic career, traveling across and shooting the vast expanse of the Southwest.

  5. Traveling across and shooting the vast expanse of the Southwest, Ansel Adams began his photographic career in 1945.

Solution:Choice (A) has two flaws. First, the introductory phrase is too long. Second, the subject Ansel Adams should immediately follow the introductory phrase since it was Ansel Adams—not the year 1945—who was traveling and shooting the Southwest.

Choice (B) is incorrect because the phrase traveling across Southwest" is too far from its subject Ansel Adams. As written, the sentence seems to imply that the photographic career was traveling across and shooting the Southwest.

Choice (C) is inconsistent in verb tense. Further, it implies that Adams began his photographic career after he traveled across the Southwest.

Choice (D) is awkward.

The best answer is choice (E).
