Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

Half of the people who take the GRE score above 500 and half of the people score below 500.

Column A
The average (arithmetic mean) score on the GRE ?

Column B
500

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution:Many students mistakenly think that the given information implies the average is 500. Suppose just 2 people take the test and one scores 700 (above 500) and the other scores 400 (below 500). Clearly, the average score for the two test-takers is not 500. The answer is (D).
