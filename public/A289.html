Q. A circle is depicted in the rectangular coordi nate system as shown. The value of x is

  1. 4

  2. 6

  3. 8

  4. 10

  5. 12

Solution: The figure shows that the circle is located between the lines y = 4 and y = –4 and that the circle is symmetric to x-axis. From this, we make two observations:

1) The center of the circle is on the x-axis.

2) The diameter of the circle is 8.

Since the center of the circle is on the x-axis, the points (2, 0) and (x, 0) must be diametrically opposite points of the circle. That is, they are end points of a diameter of the circle.
Hence, the distance between the two points, x – 2, must equal the length of the diameter.
Hence, x – 2 = 8. Adding 2 to both sides of this equation, we get x = 10. The answer is D.
