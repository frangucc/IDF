Q.   In the figure below, triangle ABC is isosceles with base AC. If x = 60˚, then AC =

  1. 2

  2. 3

  3. 4

  4. 14/3

  5. √30

Solution: Since the triangle is isosceles, with base AC, the base angles are congruent (equal). That is, A = C. Since the angle sum of a triangle is 180, we get

A + C + x = 180

Replacing C with A and x with 60 gives

A + A + 60 = 180

     2A + 60 = 180

              2A = 120

                A = 60

Hence, the triangle is equilateral (all three sides are congruent). Since we are given that side AB has length 4, side AC also has length 4. The answer is (C).
