Q. In the following sentence, part or all of the sentence is underlined. The answer-choices offer five ways of phrasing the underlined part. If you think the sentence as written is better than the alternatives, choose A, which merely repeats the underlined part; otherwise choose one of the alternatives.

Unless you maintain at least a 2.0 GPA, you will not graduate medical school.

  1. you will not graduate medical school.

  2. you will not be graduated from medical school.

  3. you will not be graduating medical school.

  4. you will not graduate from medical school.

  5. you will graduate medical school.

Solution:Choice (A) is incorrect. In this context, graduate requires the word from: you will not graduate from medical school."

The use of the passive voice in choices (B) and (C) weakens the sentence.

Choice (D) is the answer since it uses the correct idiom graduate from.

Choice (E) changes the meaning of the sentence and does not correct the faulty idiom.
