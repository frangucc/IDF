Q.   A bowl contains one marble labeled 0, one marble labeled 1, one marble labeled 2, and one marble labeled 3. The bowl contains no other objects. If two marbles are drawn randomly without replacement, what is the probability that they will add up to 3?

  1. 1/12

  2. 1/8

  3. 1/6

  4. 1/4

  5. 1/3

Solution:The following list shows all 12 ways of selecting the two marbles:

(0, 1)

(1, 0)

(2, 0)

(3, 0)

(0, 2)

(1, 2)

(2, 1)

(3, 1)

(0, 3)

(1, 3)

(2, 3)

(3, 2)

The four pairs in bold are the only ones whose sum is 3.
Hence, the probability that two randomly drawn marbles will have a sum of 3 is

4/12 = 1/3
The answer is (E).
