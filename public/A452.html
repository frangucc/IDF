Q.   For all real numbers a and b, where a × b ≠ 0, let a◊b = ab − 1, which of the following must be true?

I. a◊b = b◊a

II.
 a◊a 
 a 
 = 1◊1

III. (a◊b)◊c = a◊(b◊c)

  1. I only

  2. II only

  3. III only

  4. I and II only

  5. I and III only

Solution:a◊b = ab – 1 = ba – 1 = b◊a. Thus, I is true, which eliminates (B) and (C).

 a◊a 
 a 
 = 
 aa − 1  
 a 
 ≠ 1 × 1 − 1 

= 1 − 1 = 0 =1◊1

Thus, II is false, which eliminates (D).

(a◊b)◊c = (ab − 1)◊c = (ab − 1)c − 1 

= abc − c − 1 ≠ a◊(bc − 1) 

= a(bc − 1) − 1 = abc − a − 1 = a◊(b◊c).

Thus, III is false, which eliminates (E).
Hence, the answer is (A).
