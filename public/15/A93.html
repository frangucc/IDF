Q.   If the sum of three consecutive integers is odd, then the first and the last integers must be

  1. odd, even

  2. odd, odd

  3. even, odd

  4. even, even

  5. none of the above

Solution:Let the three consecutive integers be x, x + 1, and x + 2. The sum of these integers is 3x + 3. According to the question, this sum is odd. Hence 3x + 3 is odd. Recall that if the sum of two integers is odd, then one of the integers is odd and the other one is even. Since 3 in the expression 3x + 3 is odd, 3x must be even. Now, recall that the product of two numbers is odd only when one of the numbers is odd and the other is even. So x must be even. If x is an even number, then x + 2 is also even. Thus, the first and the last integers must both be even. The answer is (D).
