Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

Column A
The average of 2x – 5,

4x + 6, and 5 – 6x

Column B
The average of –1, 3, 4, and 10

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: The average of N numbers is their sum divided by N. Forming the average in Column A yields

Average = 
 sum 
 N 
 

= 
 2(x − 5) + (4x + 6) + (5 − 6x) 
 3 
 = 
 6 
 3 
 = 2

Forming the average in Column B yields

Average = 
 sum 
 N 
 = 
 −1 + 3 + 4 + 10 
 4 
 = 
 16 
 4 
 = 4

Thus, Column B is larger, and the answer is (B).
