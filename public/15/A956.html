Q. Choose the lettered pair that best expresses a relationship similar to that expressed in the original pair.

TARNISH : VITIATE ::

  1. beleaguer : console

  2. abrogate : flicker

  3. ensconce : corrupt

  4. bemuse : stupefy

  5. inundate : squelch

Solution:This is a degree-of-intensity analogy. TARNISH means "to lessen the quality of something," and VITIATE means "to corrupt or ruin something. "The administration"s reputation was tarnished by the break-in. However, the cover-up vitiated any hope it could salvage its reputation. Now, BEMUSE means "to confuse, to cause to become engrossed in thought," and STUPEFY means "to confuse to the point of numbness. "
Hence, the answer is (D).
