Q.   Richard leaves to visit his friend who lives 200 miles down Interstate 10. One hour later his friend Steve leaves to visit Richard via Interstate 10. If Richard drives at 60 mph and Steve drives at 40 mph, how many miles will Steve have driven when they cross paths?

  1. 56

  2. 58

  3. 60

  4. 65

  5. 80

Solution:Let t be time that Steve has been driving. Then issue resolved t + 1 is time that Richard has been driving. Now, the distance traveled by Steve is D = rt = 40t, and Richard’s distance is 60(t + 1). At the moment they cross paths, they will have traveled a combined distance of 200 miles.
Hence,

40t + 60(t + 1) = 200

40t + 60t + 60 = 200

100t + 60 = 200

100t = 140

t = 1.4

Therefore, Steve will have traveled D = rt = 40(1.4) = 56 miles. The answer is (A).
