The amount by which the number of cars in 1990 exceeded the number of accidents in 1991 was approximately

  1. 0.3 million

  2. 0.7 million

  3. 1.0 million

  4. 1.7 million

  5. 2.5 million

Solution:From the bottom chart, there were 2 million cars in 1990; and from the top chart, there were 340 thousand accidents in 1991. Forming the difference yields

2,000,000 – 340,000 = 1,660,000

Rounding 1.66 million off yields 1.7 million. The answer is (D).
