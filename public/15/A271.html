Q. The perimeter of a square is equal to the perimeter of a rectangle whose length and width are 6m and 4m, respectively. The side of the square is

  1. 3m

  2. 4m

  3. 5m

  4. 6m

  5. 7m

Solution:The length of the rectangle is 6m and the width of the rectangle is 4m. From the standard formula for the perimeter of a rectangle, we get

P = 2L + 2W = 2(6m) + 2(4m) = 20m

Now, the formula for the perimeter of a square is 4x, where x represents the length of a side of the square. Since we are given that the perimeter of the square is equal to that of the rectangle, we write

4x = 20m

x = 20m/4 = 5m
The answer is C.
