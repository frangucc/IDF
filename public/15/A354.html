Q. If 2x + y > m and 2y + x < n, then x – y must be greater than

  1. m + n

  2. m – n

  3. mn

  4. 2m + n

  5. n – m

Solution: Aligning the system of inequalities vertically yields

2x + y > m

2y + x < n

Multiplying both sides of the bottom inequality by –1 and flipping the direction of the inequality yields

–2y – x > –n

Adding this inequality to the top inequality yields

(2x + y) + (–2y – x) > m – n

(2x – x) + (–2y + y) > m – n

x – y > m – n
The answer is B.
