Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

 3x − 1 
 2 
 − 
 x + 2 
 4

 x − 1 
 2 
 − 
 3x − 4 
 4 

Column A
 x + 1 
 2 

Column B
x

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: First, clear fractions by multiplying the equation
 3x − 1 
 2 
 − 
 x + 2 
 4 
 = 
 x − 1 
 2 
 − 
 3x − 4 
 4 
by the LCD, 4:

2(3x – 1) – (x + 2) = 2(x – 1) – (3x – 4)

Distributing yields

6x – 2 – x – 2 = 2x – 2 – 3x + 4

Combining like terms yields

5x – 4 = –x + 2

Adding x and 4 to both sides of the equation yields

6x = 6

Dividing both sides of the equation by 6 yields

x = 1

Hence,
 x + 1 
 2 
 = 
 1 + 1 
 2 
 = 
 2 
 2 
 = 1. Thus, the columns are equal, and the answer is (C).
