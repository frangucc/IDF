Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

In each of the years 1996 and 1997, Easy Tax Software sold 2.1 million copies more than in the previous year.

Column A
The percent increase in the number of copies sold in 1996 over the previous year

Column B
The percent increase in the number of copies sold in 1997 over the previous year

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: Remember that the percent increase is the absolute increase divided by the original amount. Let x be the total number of copies sold in 1995. Then the percent increase for 1996 is

 original amount 
 = 
 2.1 
 x 
. The number copies sold in 1996 is x + 2.1. Forming the percent increase for 1997 yields

 original amount 
 = 
 2.1 
 x + 2.1 
. Since the numerators of the two fractions are the same but the denominator of the fraction for 1997 is larger, the fraction is smaller.
Hence, Column A is larger, and the answer is (A).
