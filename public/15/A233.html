Q. In the figure below, the length of QS is

  1. √51

  2. √61

  3. √69

  4. √77

  5. √89

Solution:The length of PR is PR = 3 + 5 = 8. Applying the Pythagorean Theorem to triangle PRS yields

82 + (PS)2 = 102

Squaring yields 64 + (PS)2 = 100

Subtracting 64 from both sides yields (PS)2 = 36

Taking the square root of both sides yields PS = √36 = 6

Now, applying the Pythagorean Theorem to triangle PQS yields (QS)2 = 52 + 62

Squaring and adding yields (QS)2 = 61

Taking the square root of both sides yields QS = √61
The answer is B.
