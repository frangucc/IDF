Q.   A hat contains 15 marbles, and each marble is numbered with one and only one of the numbers 1, 2, 3. From a group of 15 people, each person selects exactly 1 marble from the hat.

Numbered Marble

Number of People Who Selected The Marble

1

4

2

5

3

6

What is the probability that a person selected at random picked a marble numbered 2 or greater?

  1. 5/15

  2. 9/15

  3. 10/15

  4. 11/15

  5. 1

Solution:There are 11 (= 5 + 6) people who selected a number 2 or number 3 marble, and there are 15 total people.
Hence, the probability of selecting a number 2 or number 3 marble is 11/15, and the answer is (D).
