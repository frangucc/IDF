Q.   Except for the first two numbers, every number in the sequence –1, 3, 2, . . . is the sum of the two immediately preceding numbers. How many numbers of this sequence are even?

  1. none

  2. one

  3. two

  4. three

  5. more than three

Solution:Since "every number in the sequence –1, 3, 2, . . . is the sum of the two immediately preceding numbers," the forth term of the sequence is 5 = 3 + 2. The first 12 terms of this sequence are

–1, 3, 2, 5, 7, 12, 19, 31, 50, 81, 131, 212, . . .

At least four numbers in this sequence are even: 2, 12, 50, and 212. The answer is (E).
