Q.   In the two-digit number x, both the sum and the difference of its digits is 4. What is the value of x?

  1. 13

  2. 31

  3. 40

  4. 48

  5. 59

Solution:Since the sum of the digits is 4, x must be 13, 22, 31, or 40. Further, since the difference of the digits is 4, x must be 40, 51, 15, 62, 26, 73, 37, 84, 48, 95, or 59. We see that 40 and only 40 is common to the two sets of choices for x.
Hence, x must be 40. The answer is (C).
