Q.   A 30% discount reduces the price of a commodity by $90. If the discount is reduced to 20%, then the price of the commodity will be

  1. $180

  2. $210

  3. $240

  4. $270

  5. $300

Solution:Let the original price of the commodity be x. The reduction in price due to the 30% discount is 0.3x. It is given that the 30% discount reduced the price of the commodity by $90. Expressing this as an equation yields

0.3x = 90

Solving for x yields

x = 300

Hence, the original price of the commodity was $300. The value of a 20% discount on $300 is

.20(300) = 60

Hence, the new selling price of the commodity is

$300 – $60 = $240
The answer is (C).
