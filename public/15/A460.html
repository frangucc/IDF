Q.   If p = z + 1/z and q = z – 1/z, where z is a real number not equal to zero, then (p + q)(p – q) =

  1. 2

  2. 4

  3. z2

  4.  1 
     z2 

  5. z2 − 
     1 
     z2 

Solution:Since we are given that p = z + 1/z and q = z – 1/z,

p + q = (z + 1/z) + (z – 1/z)
= z + 1/z + z – 1/z = 2z

p – q = (z + 1/z) – (z – 1/z)
= z + 1/z – z + 1/z = 2/z

Therefore, (p + q)(p – q) = (2z)(2/z) = 4

The answer is (B).
