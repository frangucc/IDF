Q. −24 − (x2 − 1)2 =

  1. − x4 + 2x2 + 15

  2. − x4 − 2x2 + 17

  3. − x4 + 2x2 − 17

  4. − x4 + 2x2 − 15

  5. − x4 + 2x2 + 17

Solution: –24 – (x2 – 1)2 =

–16 – [(x2)2 – 2x2 + 1] =

–16 – [x4 – 2x2 + 1] =

–16 – x4 + 2x2 – 1 =

–x4 + 2x2 – 17
The answer is (C).

Notice that –24 = –16, not 16. This is one of the most common mistakes on the test. To see why –24 = –16 more clearly, rewrite –24 as follows:

–24 = (–1)24

In this form, it is clearer that the exponent, 4, applies only to the number 2, not to the number –1. So –24 = (–1)24 = (–1)16 = –16.

To make the answer positive 16, the –2 could be placed in parentheses:

(–2)4 = [(–1)2]4 = (–1)4 24 = (+1)16 = 16
