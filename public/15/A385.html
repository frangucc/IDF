Q.   If n is a number such that (−8)2x = 28+2x, then n =

  1. 1/2

  2. 2

  3. 3/2

  4. 4

  5. 5

Solution:Since the right side of the equation is positive, the left side must also be positive. Thus, (−8)2x is equal to

82x

This in turn can be written as

(23)2x

Multiplying the exponents gives

26x

Plugging this into the original equation gives

26x = 28+2x

Now, since the bases are the same, the exponents must be equal:

6n = 8 + 2n

Solving this equation gives

n = 2
The answer is (B).
