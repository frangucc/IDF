Q.   The age of B is half the sum of the ages of A and C. If B is 2 years younger than A and C is 32 years old, then the age of B must be

  1. 28

  2. 30

  3. 32

  4. 34

  5. 36

Solution:Let a represent the age of A and let c represent the age of C. If b represents the age of B, then according to the question b = 
 a + c 
 2 
. We are told that B is 2 years younger than A. This generates the equation a = b + 2. We know that the age of C is 32. Substituting these values into the equation b = 
 a + c 
 2 
yields b = 
 (b + 2) + 32 
 2 
. Solving this equation for b yields b = 34. The answer is (D).
