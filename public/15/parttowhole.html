PART TO WHOLE

In this type of analogy, the first word is part of the second word. The order can also be whole to part.

Example:
ATOM : MATTER ::

  1. neutron : proton

  2. vegetable : animal

  3. molecule : element

  4. component : system

  5. pasture : herd

Solution:MATTER is made up of ATOMS. Similarly, a SYSTEM is made up of COMPONENTS. The answer is (D). Choice (C) is second-best. A molecule is made of elements, but the direction here is the reverse of that in the original pair. Be wary of the pair NEUTRON : PROTON it is an eye-catcher: both words remind you of an ATOM. However, a NEUTRON is not part of a PROTON, nor vice versa.

Example:
ACTORS : TROUPE ::

  1. plotters : cabal

  2. professors : tenure

  3. workers : bourgeoisie

  4. diplomats : government

  5. directors : cast

Solution:A TROUPE is a group of ACTORS. Similarly, a CABAL is a group of PLOTTERS. The answer is (A). Be wary of the pair DIRECTORS : CAST it’s an eye-catcher: CAST reminds one of TROUPE. In fact, a TROUPE is a CAST.
