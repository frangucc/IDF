Q.   If p and q are different prime numbers and pq/2 is also a prime number, then p + q is

  1. an odd number

  2. an even number

  3. a prime number

  4. a negative number

  5. not a prime number

Solution:Since pq/2 is prime, it is an integer.
Hence, either p or q must be even; otherwise, the 2 would not cancel and pq/2 would be a fraction. The only even prime number is 2.
Hence, either p or q, but not both, must be 2. The other one is an odd prime number. Now, the sum of an even number and an odd number is an odd number. The answer is (A).
