Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

x and y are positive.

Column A
Average of x and y

Column B
Average of x2 and y2

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution: Remember, different variables can stand for the same number.
With that in mind, let x = y = 1.
Then Column A becomes
 1 + 1 
 2 
 = 1 .
And Column B becomes
 12 + 12 
 2 
 = 1 .
In this case, the columns are equal.
But if x = y = 2,
then Column A becomes
 2 + 2 
 2 
 = 2 and
Column B becomes
 22 + 22 
 2 
 = 4 .
In this case, the columns are unequal. This is a double case and therefore the answer is (D).
