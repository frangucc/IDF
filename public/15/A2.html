Q. Solve the following problems by using substitution.

If x and y are perfect squares, then which of the following is not necessarily a perfect square?

  1. x2

  2. xy

  3. 4x

  4. x + y

  5. x5

Solution:Choose x = 4 and y = 9. Then x2 = 42 = 16 which is a perfect square. (Note, we cannot eliminate x2 because it may not be a perfect square for another choice of x.)
Next, xy = 4 × 9 = 36 which is a perfect square.
Next, 4x = 4 × 4 = 16, which is a perfect square.
Next, x + y = 4 + 9 = 13, which is not a perfect square.
Hence, the answer is (D).
