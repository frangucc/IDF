Q.   If
 x + 5 
 y 
is a prime integer, which of the following must be true?

I. y = 5x

II. y is a prime integer.

III.
 x + 5 
 y 
is odd.

  1. None

  2. I only

  3. II only

  4. I and II only

  5. II and III only

Solution:If x = 1 and y = 3, then

y ≠ 5x

and

 x + 5 
 y 
 = 
 1 + 5 
 3 
 = 
 6 
 3 
 = 2,

which is prime and not odd.
Hence, Statements I and III are not necessarily true.
Next, let x = 3 and y = 4. Then y is not prime and

 x + 5 
 y 
 = 
 3 + 5 
 4 
 = 
 8 
 4 
 = 2,

which is prime.
Hence, Statement II is not necessarily true. The answer is (A).
