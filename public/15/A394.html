Q. Determine which of the two expressions below is larger, whether they are equal, or whether there is not enough information to decide.

x + 3y = 2 and
3x + y = 14

Column A
x + y

Column A
x – y

  1. Column A is larger

  2. Column B is larger

  3. Column A and Column B are equal

  4. Not enough information to decide.

Solution:Writing the system of two given equations vertically yields

x + 3y = 2

3x + y = 14

First, adding the two equations yields

4x + 4y = 16

4(x + y) = 16 by factoring out 4

x + y = 16/4 = 4 by dividing by 4

Now, consider the system of the two given equations again

3x + y = 14

x + 3y = 2

Second, subtracting the two equations yields

2x – 2y = 12

2(x – y) = 12 by factoring out 2

x – y = 12/2 = 6 by dividing by 2
The answer is (B).
