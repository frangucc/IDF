Q. In the following sentence, part or all of the sentence is underlined. The answer-choices offer five ways of phrasing the underlined part. If you think the sentence as written is better than the alternatives, choose A, which merely repeats the underlined part; otherwise choose one of the alternatives.

In the 1950’s, integration was an anathema to most Americans; now, however, most Americans accept it as desirable.

  1. to most Americans; now, however, most Americans accept it as desirable.

  2. to most Americans, now, however, most Americans accept it.

  3. to most Americans; now, however, most Americans are desirable of it.

  4. to most Americans; now, however, most Americans accepted it as desirable.

  5. to most Americans. Now, however, most Americans will accept it as desirable.
