Q.   In a small town, 16 people own Fords and 11 people own Toyotas. If exactly 15 people own only one of the two types of cars, how many people own both types of cars.

  1. 2

  2. 6

  3. 7

  4. 12

  5. 14

Solution:This is a hard problem. Let x be the number of people who own both types of cars. Then the number of people who own only Fords is 16 – x, and the number of people who own only Toyotas is 11 – x. Adding these two expressions gives the number of people who own only one of the two types of cars, which we are are told is 15: (16 – x) + (11 – x) = 15. Adding like terms yields 27 – 2x = 15. Subtracting 27 from both sides of the equation yields –2x = –12. Finally, divide both sides of the equation by –2 yields x = 6. The answer is (B).
