Q.   p and q are integers. If p is divided by 2, the remainder is 1; and if q is divided by 6, the remainder is 1. Which of the following must be true.

I. pq + 1 is even.

II. pq/2 is an integer.

III. pq is a multiple of 12.

  1. I only

  2. II only

  3. III only

  4. I and II only

  5. I and III only

Solution:Statement I is true: From "If p is divided by 2, the remainder is 1," p = 2u + 1; and from "if q is divided by 6, the remainder is 1," q = 6v + 1.
Hence, pq + 1 =

(2u + 1)(6v + 1) + 1 =

12uv + 2u + 6v + 1 + 1 =

12uv + 2u + 6v + 2 =

2(6uv + u + 3v + 1)

Since we have written pq + 1 as a multiple of 2, it is even.

Method II

Since p and q each leave a remainder of 1 when divided by an even number, both are odd. Now, the product of two odd numbers is another odd number.
Hence, pq is odd, and therefore pq + 1 is even.

Now, since pq + 1 is even, pq is odd.
Hence, pq/2 is not an integer, and Statement II is not necessarily true.
Next, Statement III is not necessarily true.
For example, if p = 3 and q = 7, then pq = 21, which is not a multiple of 12. The answer is (A).
